# Round 1 — Findings synthesis: the aesthetics of 13 AI models

**What this is.** An offline, reproducible first analysis of the "Machines of Loving Taste"
dataset (13 models × 43 domains × 2 probes, adaptive sampling, 9,262 non-refused rows).
Hypotheses were generated by three lens-agents, curated (`SHORTLIST.md`), then tested by six
statistician-agents. Every number below is reproducible from `analysis/lib/*.py` over
`data/`. Legacy pilot domains `bookcover` and `chair` are excluded from headline stats.

Verdict tags: **SUPPORTED** / **PARTIAL** / **REFUTED** / **DESCRIPTIVE** (true but not
significance-tested). "p" = permutation or exact test unless noted.

---

## The director's three hunches — scorecard

| Hunch | Verdict | One-line result |
|---|---|---|
| "Remarkable convergence across AI tastes" | **PARTIAL / reframed** | Real, but *domain-driven first* (~3× the model effect); the biggest slice is model×domain idiosyncrasy, not field-wide sameness. |
| "AIs really like Asia" | **SUPPORTED, domain-conditional** | 53% of place/cuisine favorites are Asia-coded vs 5% of overrated (10.7×); but *reverses* in media/person domains. |
| "Capability turns cliché into real taste" | **REFUTED** | 7 of 8 endpoint comparisons point the *opposite* way; survives hedge control. Stronger models converge *harder*. |

## Q1 — The structure of convergence  → **PARTIAL / reframed**
- Domain main effect beats model main effect **2.57× (favorite), 3.38× (overrated)**, both
  p=0.00005. *What* is judged matters ~3× more than *who* judges.
- **But** in absolute terms domain ≈22–25% of variance, model ≈7–9%, and the
  **model×domain interaction ≈68–70%** — the largest component. Most of "agreement" lives in
  specific model-on-specific-domain idiosyncrasy, not in either main effect.
- **Family house-style is real**: within-family > between-family agreement, favorite gap
  +0.052 (p=0.0002), overrated +0.037 (p=0.006). Cross-validated against the shipped
  favorite `overlap` matrix at r=0.99.
- Most idiosyncratic model: **Gemini 3.1 Pro** (robust across probes). Most convergent duo:
  the two Geminis (0.331 vs 0.286 runner-up). "Opus 4.8 as 2nd odd-one-out" is
  favorites-only — not stable.
- Most-unifying domains: **season, cuisine, childrensbook, poem**. Most-fracturing:
  **street, aimodel, dish, object, mathematician**. Favorite/overrated domain rankings only
  moderately correlate (r≈0.41) — a domain that unites on love can divide on scorn.

## Q2 — Agree more on rejecting than loving?  → **PARTIAL (metric-dependent)**
*This corrects an earlier overclaim of mine that it was "triple-confirmed / bankable."*
- **Modal-fraction metric**: overrated more consensual, gap +0.082, sign test p=0.024. ✓
- **Properly normalized (log-k) entropy metric**: gap +0.023, p=0.43. ✗ (coin flip.)
- The three earlier "confirmations" all used the *same* metric family, so they agreed by
  construction, not robustness. The real headline: **real but modest, and it doesn't survive
  a metric swap.**
- **The people-domain reversal** (models converge more on *loved* thinkers than *overrated*
  ones) is entropy-metric-significant (people vs cultural+places, p=0.036) but
  modal-fraction-attenuated. Concentrated in **scientific/technical thinkers** (mathematician,
  scientist, philosopher, CS, AI researcher); architects/theologians still get the shared-
  villain treatment (Gehry 92%, Freud 77%).
- Loved-and-hated-at-once entities: San Francisco (30 fav / 28 ovr), Alan Turing (32/39),
  Impressionism (29/53), Keynes (34/57).

## Q3 — Capability → less cliché?  → **REFUTED**
- Weakest-vs-strongest rung, entropy + consensus-hugging, both ladders × both hedge
  conditions = 8 comparisons: **7 against the hunch, 1 flat, 0 for.**
- Anthropic's strongest model spreads across *fewer* distinct favorites than its weakest
  (90→76); OpenAI's newest (GPT-5.6 Sol) is the *least* diverse point in its ladder.
- **Survives hedge control** — not a hedging artifact.
- Caught one real would-be-false-positive: OpenAI descriptor-richness *appears* to rise with
  capability in raw data but **reverses** once hedged rows are stripped (GPT-4o's 72% hedge
  rate was padding its token count). Exactly the trap we set the control for.
- Lone genuine exception: Anthropic's *descriptor* vocabulary richens with capability (style,
  not substance; 3 points; doesn't replicate in OpenAI).

## Q4 — Cultural geometry / "AIs like Asia"  → **SUPPORTED, domain-conditional**
- Pooled: **25.1% Asia in favorites vs 6.4% in overrated (3.9×, p=0.00005)**.
- Split resolves it cleanly:
  - **Place/cuisine-marker domains** (cuisine, city, architecture, streets, monuments,
    religious texts, board games): **53.0% vs 5.0% — 10.7×.** The hunch, *stronger* than the
    keyword estimate.
  - **Media/person domains** (film, novelist, videogame…): **4.9% vs 7.6% — reversed**
    (p=0.0066), driven almost entirely by *Zelda* and *Murakami* being each domain's
    most-famous-representative → routed to "overrated" (ties to Q5's fame mechanism).
- **Overrated is disproportionately *American*, not generically Western** — North America
  27%→46% from favorite to overrated; Western/Southern Europe barely moves.
- Temporal geography (reconfirmed): **1960s & 1920s favored, 1980s reviled.**
- Coverage 99.87%; robustness holds dropping all 44 ambiguous-flagged entities (24.9% vs 6.7%).

## Q5 — Who gets called overrated? Fame & gender  → **SUPPORTED (framed as model behavior)**
- **Fame**: ~63% of overrated volume sits on entities the favorite probe never names (Gehry
  91/0, Catcher 81/0, Mona Lisa 81/0, Monopoly 79/0). Internal "breadth" proxy: overrated
  picks are significantly more famous (6.25 vs 4.97, p<0.0001), directional (28/43). Causal
  "fame → overrated" left **RED** (needs external pageview data).
- **Gender** (transparent 200-name lookup, zero guessing, 100% coverage because the models
  only name canonical figures): **17.0% women among favorites vs 3.9% among overrated**
  (13.1pp, p<0.0001), holding in 8/11 person-domains. **Scientist flips**: 50.8% women among
  favorites (Noether/Curie/Lovelace) vs 1.8% overrated (Einstein/Tesla/Tyson). Reverses only
  in philosopher (Ayn Rand). *Describes the models' selection behavior within a small famous
  name-universe, not a general classifier.*

## Q6 — A universal AI aesthetic vocabulary?  → **SUPPORTED**
- **Models share reasons more than picks**: top-20 descriptor Jaccard 0.219 = **2.9×** the
  entity Jaccard (0.076). "Elegant" reaches 12–13/13 models by K=10; no *entity* reaches
  10/13 until K=50 (Japanese cuisine).
- **Praise and critique lexicons are nearly disjoint** — 2 of top-50 words overlap. Praise:
  *luminous, crystalline, harmonious, timeless, honest.* Critique: *shallow, hollow, tedious,
  spectacle.* "Iconic" (91 ovr / 12 fav) and "polished" (112/5) are critique tells.
- Corrections: "elegant #1 for 13/13" is actually **12/13** (Opus 4.5's top word is "honest");
  the family-vocabulary effect is K-sensitive and no stronger than family clustering in picks.

## Cross-cutting methodological findings (matter beyond any single question)
1. **Entropy normalization quirk (confirmed by 2 independent agents + source).** The shipped
   pipeline normalizes entropy by **log(n samples)**, not log(k distinct picks) — confirmed by
   the site's own caption (`src/report.js:506`). Because adaptive sampling gives *varied* cells
   more samples, the denominator varies with dispersion, coupling sample-count to the metric.
   Defensible but non-standard; it's why site entropy (~0.21) and this analysis's log-k entropy
   (~0.46) differ. **Action item: decide whether the live site's entropy visuals should switch
   to log-k, or keep log(n) with clearer labeling.**
2. **Metric choice changes conclusions** (Q2). Any single-metric agreement claim should be
   re-run under a second metric before it's trusted.
3. **Extractor confound** (Q6). `descriptors` are Haiku's paraphrase, not the models' verbatim
   words — some vocabulary convergence may be the extractor's. A future collection should keep
   verbatim adjectives.

## What we'd collect next (future-data asks)
- **External fame proxy** (Wikipedia pageviews) → makes "overrated = most famous" causal, not
  circular (Q5, Q4-media reversal).
- **Verified creator metadata** (birth country, gender, era) → upgrades Q4/Q5 from hand-built
  lookups (YELLOW) to GREEN.
- **A non-aesthetic control task** (same models, "favorite ___" on neutral prompts) → tests
  whether "elegant"-style vocabulary is aesthetics-specific or a general LLM tic (Q6).
- **Verbatim descriptor capture** → removes the extractor confound.
- **More rungs per family / more single-model families as ladders appear** → the capability
  question (Q3) rests on only 3–4 points per family; it deserves more power before "refuted"
  is final.

## The three findings I'd stake the most on
1. **AI aesthetic convergence is domain-compelled, not uniform** (Q1) — robust, cross-validated.
2. **There is a shared *language* of AI taste, sharper than the shared *objects* of it** (Q6) —
   models diverge on what's beautiful, converge on how to praise it; "iconic" is an insult.
3. **The capability→taste hunch is refuted** (Q3) — survives its own hedging control; the
   honest, useful negative result.
